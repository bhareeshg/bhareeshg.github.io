ABOUT ME

I am currently a post-doctoral fellow at Indiana University, Bloomington. My research is centered on the dynamics of N-body systems with applications to the formation, evolution, and habitability of planetary systems. I collaborate with both observers and theoreticians to better understand the peculiar new worlds that are being discovered. I also study the dynamics of compact systems which are potential sources of gravitational wave mergers. Previously I was a post-doctoral fellow at Technion-Israel Institute of Technology, Haifa. I got my doctorate in physics from Georgia Institute of Technology in 2023. Before that I got my undergraduate degrees in physics and computer science from BITS-Pilani.
